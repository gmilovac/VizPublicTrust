Welcome to the Trust Profile Explorer

This visualization uses a UMAP (Uniform Manifold Approximation and Projection) algorithm to plot U.S. government agencies based on the similarity of their trust dimension profiles.

  • Similarity: Agencies positioned closer together have more similar scores across the five core trust dimensions (Competence, Reliability, Ethics, Sincerity, Benevolence).
  • X-Axis (Overall Trust): Agencies further to the right have a higher overall average trust score across the five dimensions. Note that while the X-axis correlates highly with overall trust, individual trust dimensions may still vary significantly.
  • Color (Warmth): The dot color represents the agency's perceived warmth. The scale goes from bright red (High Warmth) to icy aqua blue (Low Warmth/Cold).
  • Border Thickness (Dominance): The black outline thickness represents the agency's perceived Dominance. Thicker lines mean higher Dominance.

To explore the raw data and compare specific agencies directly, switch to the Radial/Bar Views tab at the top.

You can also use the info buttons next to the plot for metric definitions and a list of all agency full names.

Metric Definitions

Trust: Overall confidence (X-Axis).
Warmth: Perceived friendliness (Color).
Dominance: Perceived power (Border Thickness).
5 Dimensions of Trust:
  • Competence: Ability to execute.
  • Reliability: Consistency.
  • Ethics: Moral principles.
  • Sincerity: Honesty.
  • Benevolence: Care for public interest.
